Work Term 2

Software Developer: Camis Inc

For my second work term, I was able to secure a position as a software developer at Camis Inc.

Camis Inc is a software company based in Guelph, Ontario. They provide accomodation software to parks for things like campground reservations, backcountry camping reservations, marina reservations, equipment rentals, Point of Sale services, inventory management, and more. They also provide call center services for making park reservations over the phone.

Camis has over a dozen clients who use their software. Among their largest clients are Parks Canada, Ontario Parks, Washington State Parks, and Michigan State Parks

The primary Camis Inc office is located in Guelph, Ontario where there is a call centre, help desk support, developers, quality assurance, and testers, but there are several other offices in the United States that just have call centre services.

My Job

At Camis, I was placed on the Sale Squad. Our team was responsible for the sales- related functionality of both the desktop and web solutions at Camis. Their desktop solution was a Microsoft .NET application using the WPF framework, and their web solution was an Angular web application.

The sales functionality for their desktop solution included the features that allowed users to configure various products, services, fees, etc, as well as the software to be used at point-of-sale terminals at park locations.

The sales functionality for their web solution was the web store and checkout portion of it. Users could add items to their cart or create reservations, and then be taken through a checkout workflow that would allow them to enter all of the necessary information including payment info, addresses, etc. to purchase items and services.

A large majority of the work I did was on the desktop solution. Working with C# was fairly different than any of the languages that I have worked with during my academic career, though Java would probably be the most similar.

The most applicable part of my studies during this work term was the runtime efficiency of various algorithms and library functions. When working with large amounts of data, inefficient code is a lot more noticeable than on a personal project or small assignment.

Learning Goals

Early in my work term, I developed 3 primary learning goals related to what I would be doing as a software developer at Camis, as well as action plans for each goal so that I had a well-defined path to achieving them. The following is an outline of my goals, how I achieved them, and how I knew that I had achieved them.

Goal 1: Problem Solving

My first learning goal was to be able to quickly and effectively fix bugs and add features based on tickets and user stories.

I was able to complete this goal by spending lots of time exploring the Camis software on my own, as well as by engaging in pair programming anc code reviews with my teammates.

By the end of my work term, I was able to fix bugs with similar speed and accuracy to some of the full-time developers at Camis. Being able to independently locate the source of problems, reproduce reported bugs, and make minor changes is a vital skill that I will carry forward with me to my future co-op placements.

Goal 2: Oral Communication

My second learning goal was to be able to effectively convey my ideas and questions in a technical but easily understandable way.

I was able to complete this goal by asking lots of questions about technical phrases and terminology used during stand-ups and meetings, and by participating as much as I could in discussions.

I can now properly formulate my questions, ideas, criticisms, and talking points in a thoughtful and technically-literate way. Being able to do these things is a key skill in any development job, and I will use my valuable learning experience from Camis to excel in my next endeavours.

Goal 3: Depth and Breadth of Understanding

My third learning goal was to have a great depth of knowledge and understanding of how both the Camis desktop and web solutions worked, how to navigate their code, etc.

By working on dozens of tickets during my time at Camis, I ended up touching many different areas of their desktop and web solutions. Through this exposure I was able to get a very good understanding of how their solutions are designed and how to properly navigate them.

On several occasions, I asked my manager for more exposure to the different technologies that Camis was using, and it paid off. I am now fairly proficient in the .NET framework and C# programming, WPF application design, Angular web development, and relational database design.

Conclusion

My work term at Camis was, in my opinion, the most valuable learning experience I've had so far. I was treated as a full member of the team, and I got to work on real production code and make meaningful contributions.

I built on many of my skills during my term at Camis, including my problem solving, communication, and developer skills. I was also able to experience what production development is really like, and built a significant understanding of the .NET framework, WPF applications, and Angular web applications.

Spending this past work term at Camis has made me much more confident in my abilities. I regularly received positive feedback from both teammates and my manager, and now I know that I have what it takes to be a great software developer.
